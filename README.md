Question 1: How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?:

Truly interpreting what a user needs from a program requires stepping into their shoes and determining what they need and/or want. If you build a huge analytics dashboard and the user just wanted a simple weather app, you have missed the point entirely. User stories do exactly what you need in order to learn what the user is looking for. They allow you to define what the user wants directly from their feedback and get a good idea of where to begin on features or program improvement.

Question 2: How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

Approaching a developing program should be done with an attitude of learning and understanding before touching anything. Learning the architecture is far more important since it gives you a vector for how to proceed with creating features or developing the program further. I hope to incorporate Agile into future development work through the Scrum style of product management. I really enjoyed the MoSCoW method to refine exactly what features are necessary and which ones are not. I also intend to use sprint planning and daily scrums for fun projects I do with my friends when running video game servers. A lot of good planning goes a long way in a small team project like this.

Question 3: What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

To me, a good team member is one who communicates and is honest about their intentions and shortcomings, etc. Someone who can admit when they need help and helps the team if needed. Far too often, people suffer in silence or think they must do it alone, letting their pride get in the way. If you’re on a good team, everyone will struggle if you do it this way. Ultimately, communication and teamwork mean the most to being a good team member in software development.
